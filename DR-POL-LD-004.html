This policy establishes guidelines for identifying, approving, participating in, and evaluating External Training programs to ensure effective use of resources and alignment with organizational goals.

2.1   In Scope

This policy applies to all Employees nominated for or requesting participation in training programs conducted by external providers and fully or partially funded by the Company. This includes:

  • Workshops
  • Seminars
  • Certifications
  • Conferences
  • Online courses
  • Licensures
  • Other similar professional development activities

2.2   Out of Scope

This policy does not apply to the following:

  • Training programs conducted by the Learning & Development (L&D) team
  • Educational assistance
  • External Training not funded by the Company

For the avoidance of doubt, all provisions related to cost, reimbursement, and repayment obligations shall apply only to the portion of training costs funded by the Company, unless otherwise explicitly stated in the Training Bond Agreement.

  • External Training Provider – A third-party organization or individual delivering training services outside the Company.
  • External Training – Any learning activity conducted by an external provider, whether in-person or virtual.
  • Vendor Sourcing – The process of identifying, evaluating, and selecting External Training providers.
  • Training Bond Agreement – A formal agreement outlining Employee obligations following Company-sponsored training.
  • Sponsoring Department – The department responsible for initiating or supporting the training request.
  • Knowledge Transfer – The structured process of sharing knowledge, skills, and insights gained from training with other Employees or teams.
  • RICA Helpdesk – Internal platform for request, issues, and change assistance.

The organization supports Employee development through participation in External Training programs that enhance job performance, professional growth, and organizational capability. All External Training engagements must align with business objectives, follow proper approval processes, and uphold the organization's standards of professionalism and accountability.

4.1   Employee Eligibility

Employees must meet the following criteria to be eligible for External Training sponsorship:

  • Full-time regular employment status at the time of application and throughout the training period.
  • No active disciplinary infractions or pending administrative cases, unless approved as an exception by management.
  • Satisfactory performance — at least a "Meets Expectations" rating (or equivalent) in the most recent performance evaluation, and not under a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) unless the training directly addresses identified performance gaps.
  • Sufficient tenure in role and relevance of the training to current or future responsibilities; operational requirements must also be considered.

Final eligibility is subject to approval by the Employee's immediate supervisor and the L&D Team.

4.2   Training Request & Approval

All External Training must be requested through the RICA Helpdesk and approved prior to enrollment. Approval must consider alignment with business goals, budget availability, operational impact, and training classification.

All requests must be submitted at least 30 working days before the end of the enrollment period. The approval process follows defined SLAs:

  • Request Submission – Employee submits a complete training request with required details and justification
  • Manager Approval – within 3 working days from submission
  • L&D Evaluation (relevance and vendor validation) – within 10 working days
  • Final Approval from Director and Chiefs (if applicable) – within 5 working days after manager approval and L&D review
  • Finance Review and Approval – within 10 working days after all reviews and approvals are completed
  • Signing of Training Bond – 2 days after funding decision

For training costs below Php 10,000, final approval from Director and Chief is not required; however, Manager approval, L&D evaluation, and Finance validation remain mandatory.

4.2.2   Budget Alignment and Pre-Approved Training Plans

Training programs included in the department's approved annual budget or training plan shall be considered pre-approved from a budget allocation perspective. However, all External Training requests must still undergo the standard request and approval process to ensure alignment with current business priorities, validation of training details, confirmation of operational readiness, and compliance with Procurement and Finance requirements.

Where applicable, budget-approved programs may be subject to streamlined review timelines. Any deviations from the approved budget—including changes in cost, provider, scope, or participant—must be re-evaluated and may require additional approvals.

4.3   Vendor Sourcing & Selection

External Training providers must undergo a structured sourcing and evaluation process. Selection should be based on provider reputation, industry recognition, content relevance, facilitator expertise, and cost-effectiveness. All vendor sourcing and selection activities shall comply with the organization's Procurement policies, including requirements for vendor accreditation, due diligence, contracting, and approval thresholds. L&D should maintain a preferred vendor list, and new vendors must be vetted in coordination with Procurement before engagement.

4.4   Cost & Funding

Training costs must be reviewed and approved in advance. Training costs shall include, but are not limited to:

  • Registration or enrollment fees
  • Examination fees
  • Learning materials
  • Travel and transportation expenses
  • Lodging or accommodation
  • Meal allowances
  • Other incidental expenses, provided that such expenses are clearly defined, justified, and pre-approved

All cost and funding decisions shall comply with the organization's Finance and Procurement policies. No expenses shall be incurred without prior approval and confirmation of available budget.

4.5   Employee Responsibilities

4.5.1   Knowledge Transfer

Employees must actively participate in the training, complete all requirements, and apply acquired knowledge and skills to their roles. Within five (5) working days after the conclusion of the training, employees are required to submit:

  • Proof of completion
  • A summary of key learnings or takeaways, including how the training will support their role and contribute to the organization

For Type B (Certification-Based) and Type C (Licensure/High-Impact) training, employees may also be required to conduct a structured knowledge transfer session (e.g., presentation, workshop, or documentation) as determined by the L&D Team or Department Head.

4.5.2   Company Representation

Employees attending External Training act as representatives of the organization and must maintain professional conduct at all times. Employees must protect confidential and proprietary information. Any misconduct or inappropriate behavior during training may result in disciplinary action and may affect future training eligibility.

4.6   Training Bond & Agreement

The training bond and agreement protects the organization's investment in Employee development. Employees participating in External Training will be required to enter into a training bond or agreement outlining the required service period after training and conditions under which training costs may be reimbursed to the Company.

4.6.3   Repayment Obligations

The Employee agrees to reimburse the Company for training-related costs under the following conditions:

  • Voluntary resignation before completion of the service obligation
  • Termination for Just Cause under Article 297 of the Labor Code of the Philippines
  • Failure to complete the training without a valid documented reason
  • Other Employee-related reasons including non-attendance without valid reason, failure to complete required training outputs, withdrawal without prior approval, or failure to submit post-training requirements

Repayment shall be calculated on a pro-rated basis proportionate to the remaining unserved bond period:

Remaining Service PeriodRepayment Amount
100% (before training completion or immediately after)100% of the total cost
75% of the bond period remaining75% of the total cost
50% of the bond period remaining50% of the total cost
25% of the bond period remaining25% of the total cost
Completed bond periodNo repayment required

The Company reserves the right to deduct any outstanding amount from the Employee's final pay, subject to applicable labor laws. If the final pay is insufficient, the Employee agrees to settle the remaining balance within thirty (30) calendar days from the date of separation.

4.6.4   Exceptions

Repayment shall not be required if separation is due to termination by the Company for Authorized Causes under Article 298 of the Labor Code of the Philippines (e.g., redundancy, retrenchment, cessation of business), medical incapacity, or other valid and documented reasons beyond the Employee's control.

4.6.5   Stacking of Bond Terms

If the Employee enters into a new Training Bond while an existing bond period is still active, the bond periods shall run concurrently; however, each bond shall remain independently enforceable. In the event of separation while multiple bonds are active, the Employee shall be liable to reimburse the outstanding balance for each bond separately.

4.6.6   Continuity of the Agreement

The Agreement shall remain in force even in the event of transfer, reassignment, or promotion within the Company or any of its affiliates or subsidiaries, unless otherwise waived in writing by the Company.

4.6.7   Use of Acquired Knowledge and Conflict of Interest

The Employee agrees that any knowledge, skills, certifications, or qualifications acquired through the Training covered by the Bond shall be primarily used for the benefit of Dermorepubliq Corporation during the Bond Period. Any external use that results in a direct conflict of interest or competitive disadvantage must be disclosed and may require prior written approval from the Company.

4.7   Non-Attendance and Withdrawal

Employees are expected to attend and complete all approved External Training. Failure to attend or complete without valid documented justification may result in reimbursement of costs and may affect future training eligibility. Guidelines for withdrawal:

  • Withdrawal before start date – must be formally communicated and approved. No penalty applies if cancellation is within the provider's allowable period and no costs are incurred.
  • Late withdrawal or no-show – where cancellation occurs beyond the provider's allowable period, may result in full or partial forfeiture of training fees.
  • Withdrawal after training has commenced – must be justified and approved. Costs incurred up to the point of withdrawal shall be assessed accordingly.

All cases of non-attendance or withdrawal shall be reviewed and documented. Repeated occurrences may impact eligibility for future External Training opportunities.

This policy is governed by the Learning and Development (L&D) Team in coordination with Department Heads, Finance, and Procurement to ensure proper implementation, consistency, and alignment with organizational objectives.

6.1   Compliance

Compliance with this policy is monitored through audit checks and training records, including validation of eligibility, adherence to approval workflows, vendor sourcing requirements, financial controls, and completion of post-training and knowledge transfer requirements. Regular reviews shall be conducted to assess policy effectiveness, identify gaps, and implement corrective actions where necessary.

6.2   Enforcement

Enforcement is applied through established approval controls, documentation requirements, and accountability mechanisms. Non-compliance—including unauthorized training participation, failure to meet requirements, or misuse of training funds—may result in cost recovery, disqualification from future External Training opportunities, and disciplinary measures in accordance with Company policies. Continuous non-compliance will be escalated to management for appropriate action.

This Policy serves as the governing document for all External Training activities. All related procedures, agreements, and supporting documents must align with and operate within the provisions of this Policy. In case of any inconsistency, this Policy shall prevail.
