New Hire Onboarding Journey

Reviewer/s

April 1, 2026

This policy establishes a structured, consistent, and compliance-controlled framework for onboarding new employees. It ensures all new hires are effectively integrated into the organization, equipped with the necessary knowledge and tools, and aligned with company values, policies, and performance expectations.

This policy aims to:

  • Ensure compliance with legal, regulatory, and organizational requirements
  • Provide a consistent onboarding experience across all departments
  • Accelerate employee readiness and productivity
  • Reinforce company culture, values, and standards

This policy applies to all full-time, part-time, contract, and temporary employees joining the organization, regardless of role or department.

  • Onboarding – The end-to-end process of integrating a new employee into the organization, covering all phases from pre-boarding through the first one hundred eighty (180) days of employment.
  • Orientation Phase – A structured, short-term onboarding phase conducted during the employee's initial days, focused on company introduction, policies, compliance training, and system access. This phase is only one component of the overall onboarding journey.
  • Integration Phase – Role-specific onboarding, guided work exposure, and initial application of skills.
  • Early Performance Phase – The period up to the employee's third (3rd) month focused on development and adjustment.
  • Sustained Performance Phase – The period from the fourth (4th) to sixth (6th) month focused on performance consistency.
  • Compliance Training – Mandatory training required for legal, regulatory, safety, or operational purposes.
  • Conditional Pass – Temporary progression with outstanding requirements subject to a corrective action plan.
  • Endorsement – Formal approval for progression to the next onboarding phase or independent deployment.
  • Non-Endorsement – Failure to meet onboarding requirements.

The onboarding of all new hires shall follow a structured, documented, and compliance-controlled process spanning from pre-boarding through the first one hundred eighty (180) days of employment. All stakeholders—including L&D, HR, hiring managers, and support teams—are required to comply with this policy.

Completion of onboarding requirements is mandatory. Completion of the Orientation Phase alone does not constitute completion of onboarding. No new hire shall be endorsed to the next onboarding phase or operational deployment unless all applicable requirements have been completed and validated.

Endorsement must be formally approved by:

  • L&D, for training completion and onboarding readiness
  • HR, for documentation and compliance validation
  • Hiring Manager, for role readiness and operational alignment

Any endorsement made without proper approval shall be considered non-compliant. L&D shall own the onboarding framework, standards, and learning design, while HR shall govern lifecycle execution, compliance, and documentation across all phases.

4.1   General Principles

To uphold the principles of this policy, all stakeholders must comply with the following mandatory requirements:

  • Completion of Mandatory Training – All new employees must complete assigned compliance, security, policy, and role-specific training within the established timelines.
  • Documentation Submission – Required employment, legal, and regulatory documents must be submitted accurately and on time.
  • Manager-Driven Onboarding Execution – Hiring Managers must create and implement a structured onboarding plan, ensure system access is set up, conduct regular check-ins, and monitor progress.
  • Attendance & Participation – Employees must actively participate in orientation sessions, training activities, and team integration meetings.
  • Recordkeeping – L&D shall maintain training-related records; HR shall maintain onboarding documentation, personnel records, and compliance tracking.

The onboarding journey spans from pre-boarding through the first one hundred eighty (180) days of employment, designed to progressively integrate new hires through defined phases. The Orientation Phase represents only the initial stage and must not be interpreted as the complete onboarding process.

5.1   Pre-Boarding Phase

Administrative and operational preparation conducted prior to the employee's start date. At a minimum, this phase shall include:

  • Confirmation of onboarding plan, schedule, and role requirements
  • Completion of pre-employment documentation
  • System and learning platform setup
  • Preparation of onboarding materials and logistics
  • Communication of onboarding expectations and schedule

5.2   Orientation Phase (Mandatory Compliance Phase)

The organization's initial onboarding phase focused on compliance, company orientation, and foundational training. This phase is mandatory and shall not be bypassed. Exceptions due to urgent operational requirements must be formally justified and approved by HR and L&D, and documented for compliance.

Completion of the Orientation Phase is a prerequisite to progression to the Integration Phase, except for approved Conditional Pass cases. Detailed procedures, schedules, and execution guidelines are defined in the corresponding SOP.

5.3   Integration Phase

Transitions the new hire into operational responsibilities through structured onboarding and guided work application. At a minimum, this phase shall include:

  • Role-specific onboarding covering responsibilities, workflows, tools, and performance expectations
  • Introduction to team structure and stakeholders
  • Guided task execution (e.g., nesting, shadowing, supervised work)
  • Regular check-ins and feedback
  • Documented evaluation of readiness

5.4   Early Performance Phase

Covers the period up to the employee's third (3rd) month, focusing on development and adjustment.

5.5   Sustained Performance Phase

Covers the fourth (4th) to sixth (6th) month, focusing on performance consistency, coaching, and readiness for regularization.

Execution and ownership of onboarding activities across phases shall follow the governance structure defined in this policy and the RASCI matrix.

6.1   Orientation Mandatory Components

  • Company orientation and culture alignment
  • HR policies, Code of Conduct, and workplace expectations
  • Compensation and benefits orientation
  • System onboarding and access setup
  • Mandatory e-learning modules and compliance training
  • Required onboarding documentation and acknowledgments
  • Post-training assessments

6.2   Compliance Training Requirements

Mandatory compliance training must be completed within prescribed timelines. No new hire shall be deployed to production areas, operational environments, or assigned to regulated tasks without prior completion of the required minimum compliance training. At a minimum, the following must be completed prior to deployment:

  • Code of Conduct
  • Occupational Safety and Health (OSH)
  • Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP)

Additional role-specific compliance requirements may apply.

6.3   Orientation Completion Requirements

The Orientation Phase shall be considered complete only when all required orientation activities have been completed and validated, including:

  • Attendance in all required sessions (subject to approved exceptions)
  • Completion of all required training and courses
  • Achievement of required passing standards in assessments
  • Completion of all required onboarding documentation
  • Compliance with attendance, training, and conduct expectations

6.4   Orientation Endorsement Status

At the end of the Orientation Phase, new hires shall be assigned one of the following:

  • Pass – All requirements completed; eligible to proceed
  • Conditional Pass – Minor deficiencies; allowed to proceed with a documented corrective action plan
  • Fail – Requirements not met; not eligible for progression without remediation

No endorsement shall be granted if the minimum compliance requirements are not satisfied.

Execution of role-specific onboarding may be facilitated by L&D or Hiring Managers, with L&D ensuring adherence to defined onboarding standards. Subsequent onboarding phases shall include:

  • Role-specific onboarding and training
  • Guided work application and support
  • Performance monitoring and coaching
  • Formal probationary evaluations

All activities must be documented in accordance with company standards. Detailed procedures, requirements, and execution guidelines shall be defined in the corresponding SOPs.

All onboarding activities across all phases must be properly documented to ensure compliance, auditability, and effective tracking of employee progress. This includes, but is not limited to:

  • Training completion records
  • Assessment results
  • Attendance and participation records
  • Signed onboarding and training acknowledgment forms
  • Role-specific onboarding documentation
  • Integration and nesting feedback records
  • Performance plans, coaching logs, and evaluation documentation

L&D shall maintain training-related records. All onboarding records shall be forwarded to HR for personnel file maintenance, compliance tracking, and audit purposes. Failure to maintain or submit required documentation may result in delays in endorsement, progression, or employee regularization.

10.1   Enforcement

  • Deployment Restriction – New hires who have not completed required compliance training shall not access production areas, equipment, or regulated work environments.
  • Non-Completion Alerts – Notifications and reminders shall be issued when onboarding requirements, training, or documentation are overdue.
  • Escalation Protocols – Persistent non-compliance shall be escalated to the Hiring Manager, Department Head, and/or HR and L&D leadership.
  • Progression Restriction – New hires who fail to complete mandatory requirements may be restricted from progression to the next phase or independent work.
  • Endorsement Hold – Endorsement may be withheld until all required activities, training, and documentation are completed and validated.
  • Corrective Action – Failure to comply with mandatory onboarding requirements may result in corrective action in accordance with company policy.
