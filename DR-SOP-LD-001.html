This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines the standardized procedures for conducting role-specific onboarding and nesting activities during the Integration Phase of the onboarding journey.

It ensures that new hires acquire the knowledge, skills, and tools necessary for effective job performance through structured onboarding, guided work application, and documented evaluation, and are properly prepared for progression to the Early Performance Phase.

2.1   In Scope

This SOP applies to all hiring managers, department heads, role trainers, mentors, Human Resources (HR) personnel, and new individual contributors participating in the onboarding process.

2.2   Out of Scope

This SOP does not apply to new people leader employees, consultants, external partners, third-party personnel, and interns.

These roles may follow separate or modified onboarding procedures, as applicable.

2.3   Process Start and End

This SOP is triggered once the new hire has been endorsed for the Integration Phase by the Trainer.

This SOP ends upon the Hiring Manager's endorsement, extension, or non-endorsement of the new hire and progression to the Early Performance Phase, or referral to HR for appropriate action.

7.1   Integration Handoff and Review
  • The Hiring Manager shall acknowledge endorsement of the new hire from the Orientation Phase.
  • The Hiring Manager shall review the Training Report and onboarding results, including assessment performance, attendance, and identified gaps.
  • Any Conditional Pass items or outstanding requirements shall be documented, along with required corrective actions and timelines.
  • Monitoring responsibility shall be aligned based on onboarding ownership:
    • L&D, for L&D-owned onboarding
    • Hiring Manager, for department-owned onboarding
7.2   Role-Specific Onboarding

The Trainer, in coordination with the Hiring Manager, shall identify which role-specific onboarding the new hire shall undertake based on the approved onboarding plan and availability of a structured onboarding curriculum.

7.2.1   L&D-Owned Role-Specific Onboarding
  • Trainer facilitates role-specific onboarding, including an overview of the department and its structure, role introduction, job-specific tools and workflows, key performance indicators, and department policies.
  • Trainer conducts other trainings as indicated in the onboarding plan.
  • Trainer facilitates post-training assessment and survey through Dermorepubliq University (Learning Management System or LMS).
  • All required post-training assessments shall meet a minimum passing score of eighty percent (80%), unless otherwise specified in the onboarding plan. Failure to meet the required passing standard shall require reassessment, coaching, or additional onboarding activities and may impact endorsement.
  • Trainer prepares Role-Specific Onboarding Acknowledgment Form.
  • New hire signs Role-Specific Onboarding Acknowledgment Form.
  • Trainer submits signed Role-Specific Onboarding Acknowledgment Form to HR compliance for recordkeeping.
  • Trainer endorses new hire to hiring manager for Nesting phase. Proceed to step 7.5.
7.2.2   Department-Owned Role-Specific Onboarding
  • Hiring manager acknowledges endorsement from the L&D trainer.
  • Hiring manager facilitates role-specific onboarding, including an overview of the department and its structure, role introduction, job-specific tools and workflows, key performance indicators, and department policies.
  • Hiring manager facilitates post-training assessment and survey through Dermorepubliq University.
  • All required post-training assessments shall meet a minimum passing score of eighty percent (80%), unless otherwise specified in the onboarding plan. Failure to meet the required passing standard may impact endorsement.
  • Hiring manager facilitates site tour and key stakeholders meet-and-greet.
  • Hiring manager prepares Role-Specific Onboarding Acknowledgment Form.
  • New hire signs Role-Specific Onboarding Acknowledgment Form.
  • Hiring manager submits signed Role-Specific Onboarding Acknowledgment Form to L&D for monitoring and HR compliance for recordkeeping. Proceed to step 7.5.
7.3   Compliance Validation
  • The Hiring Manager, in coordination with L&D and HR, shall verify completion of all required compliance training prior to operational exposure.
  • No new hire shall proceed to nesting, production areas, or regulated work environments unless required compliance training has been completed and validated.
7.4   Attendance and Participation Requirements

Attendance, punctuality, participation, and conduct expectations during the Integration Phase shall continue to be governed by the New Hire Training Agreement and applicable company policies.

The new hire is expected to:

  • Attend all scheduled onboarding activities and nesting schedules
  • Participate actively in assigned tasks, training, and coaching activities

Any instances of unexcused absence, repeated tardiness, or failure to meet participation expectations shall be documented and may impact the new hire's evaluation and endorsement status.

7.5   Nesting Phase
7.5.1   Nesting Day 1
  • Hiring manager endorses new hire to nesting buddy.
  • The new hire shall observe team workflows.
  • The new hire shall shadow and practice simple tasks.
  • The immediate supervisor assigns starter projects to the new hire.
7.5.2   Nesting Day 2 until the end of the nesting period

The Nesting Buddy shall:

  • Support knowledge transfer through peer coaching
  • Encourage questions and direct the new employee to appropriate resources
  • Share helpful tips and insights that the new employee wouldn't find in formal documentation
  • Provide context for team norms, communication styles, and cross-functional relationships
  • Notify the Hiring Manager of any major challenges or support needs

The Hiring Manager shall:

  • Conduct regular check-ins and performance discussions
  • Monitor progress and address support needs
  • Ensure that feedback and observations are documented
7.6   Conditional Pass Monitoring
  • Any gaps identified during the Orientation Phase shall be monitored and addressed during the Integration Phase.
  • Progress against required corrective actions shall be documented.
  • Unresolved gaps may impact endorsement and progression decisions.
7.7   Integration Evaluation
7.7.1   Retrospective Discussion
  • The hiring manager shall discuss what transpired during the nesting phase with the new hire.
  • The hiring manager shall go over the general nesting feedback form and discuss the recommendations and action plans to address areas for improvement.
7.7.2   Evaluation Criteria

Evaluation shall be based on documented performance, including:

  • Completion of role-specific onboarding and required assessments
  • Performance during nesting and guided work execution
  • Attendance, participation, and conduct
  • Compliance with onboarding requirements and completion of assigned corrective actions
  • Adherence to the New Hire Training Agreement
7.7.3   Integration Outcomes

Successful Integration

  • The assigned Integration Phase owner shall determine the endorsement of the new hire based on evaluation results:
    • The Trainer, for L&D-owned role-specific onboarding and nesting
    • The Hiring Manager, for department-owned role-specific onboarding and nesting
  • The assigned Integration Phase owner shall discuss the next step and expectations for the next phases of the onboarding journey with the new hire.
  • The hiring manager shall send a signed copy of the nesting feedback form to L&D and HR for monitoring and record keeping.
  • L&D files ticket in Dermo Desk to endorse new hire for probationary status.

Integration Extension

  • In case the new hire does not satisfy the requirements of the integration phase, their integration phase shall be extended for five (5) days, where they will focus on areas for improvement.
  • The hiring manager shall discuss expectations for the nesting extension and action plans to address areas for improvement.
  • The hiring manager shall inform L&D and HR teams of nesting extension for monitoring.
  • Repeat Step 7.7.2.

Unsuccessful Integration

  • In case they do not satisfy the integration extension requirements, the hiring manager will not endorse the new hire for probationary employment.
  • The hiring manager shall discuss the reasons for discontinuation of employment with the new hire.
  • The case shall be referred to HR for appropriate action in accordance with company policy.
  • The hiring manager shall inform L&D and HR teams of unsuccessful integration for monitoring and processing of end-of-employment.

  • LD-TMP-000-1.0-2025 Role-Specific Onboarding Acknowledgment Form
  • New Hire Performance Tracker
  • DermoDesk Ticketing System
9.1   Hiring Manager Training Requirements

Before facilitating role-specific onboarding and conducting integration evaluations, Hiring Managers must:

  • Be oriented on the New Hire Onboarding Journey Policy and applicable SOPs
  • Be trained on:
    • Role-specific onboarding facilitation standards
    • Use of onboarding tools and documentation (e.g., acknowledgment forms, feedback forms)
    • Conducting structured check-ins, coaching, and performance discussions
    • Evaluation criteria and endorsement decision guidelines
  • Be familiar with:
    • Compliance training requirements
    • Attendance and Training Agreement enforcement
    • Documentation and submission requirements

Hiring Managers may be required to undergo onboarding facilitation briefing or calibration sessions conducted by Learning and Development (L&D) to ensure consistency across departments.

9.2   L&D Trainer Training Requirements

Before being assigned to facilitate role-specific onboarding or manage onboarding delivery, the L&D Trainer must:

  • Observe and assist in at least one (1) onboarding cycle covering role-specific onboarding and nesting
  • Facilitate at least one (1) onboarding cycle under supervision of an L&D Supervisor or a Senior Trainer

The L&D Trainer must demonstrate competency in:

  • Facilitation of onboarding programs and training sessions
  • Administration of assessments through the Learning Management System (LMS)
  • Documentation and reporting (e.g., training reports, acknowledgment forms)
  • Monitoring onboarding progress and identifying learning gaps
  • Applying onboarding standards, evaluation criteria, and endorsement guidelines

The L&D Supervisor shall validate readiness before the Trainer independently handles onboarding activities.

9.3   Nesting Buddy Requirements
  • Be a regular employee or have at least six (6) months tenure in the company
  • Demonstrate strong performance and understanding of team processes
  • Be capable of providing effective support, coaching, and guidance to new hires

Integration evaluation shall be completed and properly documented for all eligible new hires to ensure consistency, traceability, and compliance with onboarding standards.

Failure to meet the performance standards during the Integration Phase, including unsuccessful completion of role-specific onboarding or nesting requirements, may result in an extension of the Integration Phase, non-endorsement for progression to the Early Performance Phase, or referral to Human Resources (HR) for appropriate action, as determined based on documented evaluation results.

All performance and endorsement decisions shall be based on documented assessments, feedback, and observed behaviors to ensure objectivity, fairness, and alignment with established onboarding criteria.

Non-compliance with this SOP, including failure to conduct required onboarding activities, submit mandatory documentation, or meet defined timelines, may result in corrective actions for responsible parties.

Repeated or unresolved non-compliance may be escalated to HR and senior leadership and may impact departmental audit results, performance accountability, and overall compliance standing.

This SOP shall be reviewed at least annually by the L&D Team, or as required due to changes in roles, systems, or organizational processes.

The review shall be conducted in coordination with HR and relevant stakeholders to ensure continued alignment with organizational, legal, and regulatory requirements.
