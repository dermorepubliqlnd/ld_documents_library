PURPOSE

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the required steps, responsibilities, and

standards for managing the Early Performance Phase and Sustained Performance Phase of the

onboarding journey.

This SOP ensures that new hires receive structured support, intentional performance

development, and continuous feedback throughout the probationary period, enabling them to

achieve full role proficiency and readiness for regularization.

2
SCOPE

2.1. In Scope

This SOP applies to all newly hired employees, all departments that conduct role-specific

onboarding and training, all hiring managers, supervisors, nesting buddies, Learning and

Development (L&D), and Human Resources (HR)

2.2. Out of Scope

This SOP does not apply to consultants, external partners, and interns.

2.3. Process Start and End

This SOP is triggered upon endorsement of the new hire for progression to the Early

Performance Phase, which marks the beginning of the probationary period.

This SOP ends upon completion of the Sustained Performance Phase at the employee’s

180th day, including final evaluation and endorsement outcome for regularization or

continuation of employment.

4
DEFINITIONS

Early Performance Phase – A structured period where the new hire begins applying

learned knowledge and skills to real tasks with guided support, gradually increasing

workload and expectations.

Sustained Performance Phase – A period following initial proficiency, focused on

performance consistency, continued development, and readiness for regularization.

Performance Plan – A structured, time-bound development roadmap outlining tasks, skill

milestones, workload progression, productivity targets, and learning activities.

Probationary Period – The first six (6) months of employment, corresponding to the Early

Performance Phase and Sustained Performance Phase of the onboarding journey, used to

assess overall performance, behavior, and organizational fit.

PROCEDURE

7.1. Early Performance Phase

7.1.1. Performance Planning

The Immediate Supervisor develops a performance plan for the new employee,

which includes workload targets, skills milestones, and KPI checkpoints.

The Performance Plan shall be reviewed and aligned with the Hiring Manager prior to

implementation.

The Immediate Supervisor reviews the performance plan with the new employee,

ensure understanding, alignment, and commitment.

7.1.2. 30th Day Check-In

The new employee completes the 30th day check-in survey in Dermorepubliq

University.

The Immediate Supervisor meets with the new employee to discuss the following:

o Onboarding experience

o Role and tools readiness

o Support and inclusion

The immediate supervisor coaches the new employee, setting goals for the next

check-in.

7.1.3. 60th Day Check-In

The new employee completes the 60th day check-in survey in Dermorepubliq

University.

The Immediate Supervisor meets with the new employee to discuss the following:

o Role Confidence

o Feedback and supervision

o Teamwork and collaboration

The immediate supervisor coaches the new employee, setting goals for the next

check-in.

7.1.4. 90th Day Check-In/3rd Month Probationary Review

The new employee completes the 90th day check-in survey in Dermorepubliq

University.

  • The immediate supervisor accomplishes the 3rd month Probationary Performance

Evaluation.

The immediate supervisor meets with the new employee to discuss the following:

o Integration and belonging

o Motivation and ownership

o Organizational fit

The immediate supervisor discusses the results of the probationary review with the

new employee.

The immediate supervisor coaches the new employee, setting goals for the next

check-in.

The immediate supervisor submits a copy of the accomplished probationary review

to the HR team for personnel file maintenance and compliance tracking.

The Hiring Manager shall remain informed of progress and may provide guidance

as necessary.

7.2. Sustained Performance Phase

7.2.1. 120th Day Check-In

The new employee completes the 120th day check-in survey in Dermorepubliq

University.

The Immediate Supervisor meets with the new employee to discuss the following:

o Development conversations

o Stretch and progress

o Manager and peer support

The immediate supervisor shall coach the new employee, setting goals for the next

check-in.

7.2.2. 150th Day Check-In

The new employee completes the 150th day check-in survey in Dermorepubliq

University.

  • The immediate supervisor shall accomplish the 5th month Probationary Performance

Evaluation.

The immediate supervisor meets with the new employee to discuss the following:

o Recognition and value

o Well-being and workload

o Connection and culture

The Immediate Supervisor discusses evaluation results and provides coaching

based on identified strengths and areas for improvement.

The Immediate Supervisor and Hiring Manager shall review the evaluation results

prior to endorsement to ensure alignment with departmental standards and

expectations.

The Department Head, if applicable, may be involved for alignment on final

endorsement decisions.

All endorsement decisions shall be supported by documented evaluations,

coaching logs, and observed performance.

The Immediate Supervisor submits the completed evaluation to HR for personnel file

maintenance and compliance tracking.

The outcome of the probationary evaluation, including regularization, extension, or non-

continuation of employment, shall be determined in accordance with the Human

Resources (HR) Probationary Performance Review Policy and/or applicable SOPs.

7.2.3. 180th Day Check-In

The new employee completes the 180th day check-in survey in Dermorepubliq

University.

The immediate supervisor meets with the new employee to discuss the following:

o Confirmation and role readiness

o Career path and development opportunities

o Reflection on onboarding experience

This check-in serves as a post-evaluation engagement and transition discussion

and does not replace the formal probationary evaluation conducted during the

150th day.

The Immediate Supervisor shall ensure that any remaining development actions or

transition plans are documented and aligned with the employee’s next phase of

employment.

Coaching and development support may continue as needed beyond onboarding

completion.

TRAINING REQUIREMENTS

Supervisors and managers responsible for conducting performance discussions and evaluations

must be adequately equipped with the necessary coaching and performance management

competencies.

At a minimum, they shall undergo foundational learning interventions that cover:

Coaching fundamentals and effective feedback delivery

Performance evaluation principles and application

Conduct of structured performance discussions

New hires shall also be guided on effective self-assessment practices to support meaningful

participation in performance discussions and evaluations.

L&D may provide or assign relevant training programs to ensure consistency in execution across

departments.

Performance evaluations, check-ins, and coaching activities shall be completed and properly

documented for all eligible new hires to ensure consistency, transparency, and compliance with

onboarding standards.

Failure to complete required check-ins, evaluations, or development actions within defined timelines

may result in delayed progression, non-endorsement for regularization, or referral to Human

Resources (HR) for appropriate action in accordance with the HR Probationary Performance Review

Policy and/or applicable SOPs.

All performance and endorsement decisions shall be based on documented evaluations, coaching

logs, and observed behaviors.

Non-compliance with this SOP, including failure to conduct required performance discussions,

submit mandatory documentation, or meet defined timelines, may result in corrective actions for

responsible parties in accordance with company policy.

Repeated or unresolved non-compliance may be escalated to HR and senior leadership and may

impact departmental performance, audit results, and accountability measures.

10
DOCUMENT REVIEW AND MAINTENANCE

This SOP shall be reviewed annually by the Learning and Development (L&D) Supervisor, or as

required, to ensure continued relevance, effectiveness, and alignment with organizational, legal,

and regulatory requirements.

The review shall be conducted in coordination with Human Resources (HR) and relevant

stakeholders.

Feedback from new hires supervisors, and managers may be incorporated to support continuous

improvement of onboarding and performance management processes.
