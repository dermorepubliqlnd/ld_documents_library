This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines the responsibilities of the Learning and Development (L&D) team in leading the Pre-Boarding and Orientation Phases of the onboarding journey, in close collaboration with Human Resources (HR) and other relevant stakeholders.

L&D serves as the process owner for onboarding delivery, while HR acts as a key partner responsible for compliance, employee documentation, and onboarding governance.

This SOP ensures that onboarding activities are properly planned, executed, documented, and aligned with organizational, legal, and regulatory requirements.

2.1   In Scope

This SOP applies to all stakeholders involved in the Pre-boarding and Orientation Phases, led by L&D and executed in close coordination with HR.

This includes:

  • L&D specialists, onboarding facilitators, and training administrators
  • HR personnel responsible for onboarding coordination, documentation, and compliance
  • Hiring Managers responsible for onboarding transition and role readiness
  • Support teams, including Information Technology (IT) and Facilities and Administration (F&A)
  • All employee types (full-time, part-time, contractual, and interns)
  • All onboarding formats (virtual, hybrid, and in-person)
  • All pre-boarding activities directly involving L&D and the full Orientation Phase

HR shall serve as the primary partner of L&D in ensuring onboarding compliance, documentation, and employee readiness, along with IT and F&A.

2.2   Out of Scope

This SOP does not apply to:

  • External consultants, vendors, and third-party personnel, who are covered under separate onboarding procedures, where applicable
  • Integration Phase onboarding activities
  • Early and Sustained Performance Phases
  • Offboarding processes
2.3   Process Start and End

This SOP is triggered upon the Receipt of the HR Onboarding Notification Email from the HR Recruitment Specialist (sent two [2] business days before the new hire's start date).

This SOP ends upon the completion of the Orientation Phase, issuance of the Training Report, and formal endorsement or non-endorsement of the new hire to the Integration Phase.

7.1   Pre-Boarding Phase

Administrative and logical setup before the orientation phase, including documentation, equipment, and training arrangements.

7.1.1   Notification and Acknowledgment
  • Trainer receives and acknowledges the onboarding notification email from the Recruitment Team.
  • Trainer confirms the onboarding plan with the hiring manager, which includes role-specific onboarding, immersion, and nesting schedule and activities.
    • Leadership roles will only include the 2-day orientation and compliance courses. Immersion may be included at the hiring manager's discretion.
    • Rank-and-File roles will follow the structured 10-day orientation and integration phases. This includes the 2-day orientation, role-specific onboarding, nesting activities, and all compliance courses.
    • Internship roles will participate in a condensed training program focused on company introduction and core responsibilities, typically within a 1-day timeline.
7.1.2   Logistics Setup
  • Trainer sets up training calendar.
  • Trainer prepares training decks, modules, and other onboarding collaterals. Trainer will coordinate with content developers if updates or revisions are needed.
  • Trainer sets up training spaces, meeting links, and virtual rooms.
  • Trainer books and confirms venue with Facilities & Admin.
7.1.3   System Setup
  • Trainer receives employee information from HR.
  • Trainer receives the new employee's assigned company email from IT.
  • Trainer sets up the profile of the new employee in Dermorepubliq University.
7.1.4   Training Setup
  • Trainer enrolls new employees in the HR Onboarding Learning Path in Dermorepubliq University.
  • Trainer creates an ILT Session and registers new employees.
  • Trainer enrolls new employees in all onboarding and courses relevant to their role.
7.1.5   Training Preparation Meeting
  • L&D manager meets with the training team to discuss training details.
  • L&D manager assigns training tasks to trainers.
  • Trainer completes training checklist.
7.1.6   Training Agreement
  • Trainer prepares and prints LD-TMP-001-1.0-2025 – New Hire Training Agreement.
  • Trainer coordinates with HR to have the new employee sign the training agreement along with their employment contract before day 1.
7.2   Orientation Phase

Formal orientation covering company culture, policies, IT access, and initial training.

7.2.1   Orientation Agenda

The Orientation Phase shall be facilitated by the Learning and Development (L&D) team in coordination with Human Resources (HR) and Information Technology (IT). The session shall begin with a structured onboarding kickoff, including welcome activities, introductions, attendance recording, and expectation setting.

The Orientation Phase shall cover the following components:

  • Company overview, including mission, vision, values, structure, and business operations
  • Employment policies, workplace expectations, compensation and benefits, and the Code of Conduct
  • System setup and access, including company credentials, communication platforms, and required tools
  • Orientation to internal systems, including the Human Resource Information System (HRIS)
  • Post-training assessments and onboarding surveys to evaluate understanding
  • Completion of onboarding acknowledgment requirements
  • Deployment or mandatory compliance training, including required courses and completion timelines

Detailed session flow, materials, and facilitation methods are defined in the HR Orientation Facilitator Guide.

7.2.2   Orientation Completion Requirements

The Orientation Phase shall be considered complete only when all of the following conditions are met:

  • Completion of all required orientation sessions, training activities, and onboarding components
  • Achievement of a minimum passing score of eighty percent (80%) in all required post-training assessments, as recorded in the Learning Management System (LMS)
  • Submission of all required onboarding documentation, including acknowledgment forms
  • Compliance with attendance, participation, and conduct requirements as defined in the New Hire Training Agreement
  • No occurrence of serious policy violations or behavioral concerns during the Orientation Phase
  • Validation and documentation of all orientation-related activities and records in the Training Report
  • Failure to meet the above requirements shall result in a Conditional Pass or Fail / Non-Endorsement, subject to evaluation based on the severity and nature of the gaps
  • Conditional Pass shall not override mandatory compliance training requirements
7.2.3   Attendance and Participation Guidelines
  • Attendance and participation requirements during the Orientation Phase shall be governed by the New Hire Training Agreement.
  • All new employees are required to comply with the terms outlined in the Training Agreement, including attendance, punctuality, participation, and conduct expectations.
  • Any instances of tardiness, early departure, or absence must be properly justified and approved in accordance with the guidelines defined in the Training Agreement.
  • Unexcused absence or failure to comply with participation requirements shall be considered non-compliance.
  • Non-compliance with the Training Agreement may impact the employee's Orientation endorsement status.
  • Attendance records shall be documented and reflected in the Training Report.
7.2.4   Orientation Endorsement Status

At the end of the Orientation Phase, the Trainer shall assign one of the following endorsement statuses:

  • Pass. All orientation requirements have been completed and validated.
  • Conditional Pass. Minor and recoverable deficiencies are present but do not prevent progression. Identified gaps must be documented and shall require monitoring and corrective action during the Integration Phase. Conditional Pass shall not apply to mandatory compliance requirements or serious behavioral concerns.
  • Fail / Non-Endorsement. Major requirements are not met. Employee is not eligible to proceed to the next onboarding phase.

All endorsement decisions must be documented in the Training Report.

7.3   Post-Orientation

Assessment of the new employee's readiness for the integration phase.

7.3.1   Training Evaluation and Reporting
  • The Trainer generates training data from Dermorepubliq University.
  • The Trainer completes and sends the Training Report to stakeholders.
7.3.2   Transition to Integration Phase
  • The Trainer informs the Hiring Manager of employees who have successfully met the Orientation Phase requirements.
  • Completion of the Orientation Phase indicates readiness to proceed to the Integration Phase and does not constitute full onboarding endorsement.
  • The Hiring Manager acknowledges the update and confirms the Integration Phase plan.
7.3.3   Conditional Pass Handling
  • In case of a Conditional Pass, the Trainer informs the Hiring Manager of the employee's status, including identified gaps and required corrective actions.
  • The Trainer communicates any incomplete compliance courses and their respective due dates.
  • Monitoring responsibility during the Integration Phase shall be assigned based on ownership of role-specific onboarding:
    • If role-specific onboarding and nesting are facilitated by L&D, the Trainer shall monitor and address identified gaps.
    • If role-specific onboarding and nesting are conducted by the Hiring Manager, the Hiring Manager shall monitor and address identified gaps.
  • The Hiring Manager shall remain informed of the employee's status regardless of ownership of onboarding execution.
7.3.4   Integration Phase Execution

The execution of role-specific onboarding and nesting shall be determined based on the availability of an approved role-specific onboarding curriculum:

  • If a curriculum is available, L&D shall facilitate role-specific training and support nesting.
  • If no curriculum is available, the Hiring Manager shall conduct role-specific onboarding and supervise nesting based on the approved onboarding plan.
7.3.5   Non-Endorsement Handling
  • The Trainer refers employees who did not meet Orientation requirements to HR for evaluation.
  • HR determines the appropriate outcome, which may include:
    • Reassignment to a subsequent onboarding batch
    • Discontinuation of employment
7.3.6   Administrative Updates and Deployment Control
  • The Trainer updates the training calendar, if needed.
  • Employees shall not be deployed to production areas, operational environments, or regulated tasks unless required compliance training has been completed within the prescribed timeline.

Before being assigned to any pre-boarding and orientation task, the L&D trainer must:

  • Observe and assist a senior L&D trainer in at least one (1) cycle of pre-boarding and orientation.
  • Lead at least one (1) cycle of pre-boarding and orientation with guidance from a senior L&D trainer.

This is to ensure that the L&D trainer is ready to take on the pre-boarding and orientation phase independently.

Failure to meet established KPIs may indicate gaps in onboarding execution or learner readiness. Repeated non-performance will be reviewed by the L&D Manager and addressed through targeted corrective actions, process improvements, or capability development.

Non-compliance with this SOP, including incomplete onboarding activities or missed mandatory training, may result in delayed onboarding clearance and escalation to HR and management.

Continued non-compliance may lead to formal review and corrective measures in accordance with company policies.

This SOP shall be reviewed at least annually or as required to ensure continued relevance, effectiveness, and alignment with organizational, legal, and regulatory requirements.

The review shall be led by the Learning and Development (L&D) Manager in coordination with Human Resources (HR) and other relevant stakeholders.

All updates, revisions, or changes to this SOP shall be subject to formal review and approval in accordance with the organization's document control procedures. All changes must be version-controlled and communicated to all affected stakeholders.
