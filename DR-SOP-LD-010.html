To establish a standardized process for documenting completion of approved external training and verifying proof of knowledge transfer, ensuring that external learning outcomes are captured, shared, traceable, and reflected in employee and training records.

2.1 In Scope

This SOP covers:

  • Documentation of completed external training programs delivered by third-party providers, including seminars, workshops, certifications, conferences, and externally hosted courses.
  • Submission and validation of proof of training completion, such as certificates, attendance records, completion emails, digital badges, transcripts, or assessment results.
  • Documentation of knowledge transfer activities conducted after external training, where required by the External Training Policy, training approval conditions, or L&D assessment.
  • Review, acceptance, record updating, and closure of post-training documentation in the official ticketing and/or training record system.
  • Responsibilities of the requesting employee, immediate supervisor, L&D, HR, and Department Head in ensuring completion of evidence and knowledge transfer proof are complete and accurate.
2.2 Out of Scope

This SOP does not cover:

  • External training request submission, evaluation, approval, funding, and enrollment processes.
  • Vendor sourcing, procurement, payment processing, reimbursement, or training bond execution.
  • Design and delivery of internal training programs that are not linked to an approved external training completion requirement.
  • Assessment of whether an employee should attend external training before approval.
  • Performance management actions unrelated to training completion documentation or knowledge transfer proof.
2.3 Process Start and End

The process begins when the employee completes an approved external training program or receives completion evidence from the third-party provider.

The process ends when L&D verifies completion evidence and knowledge transfer proof, HR or the designated records owner updates the employee training record, and the ticket or record is closed.

7.1   Ticket Submission

The L&D Manager files a ticket on behalf of the employee within five (5) working days after the employee completes the training or receives completion documentation from the provider.

7.2   Submit Proof of Completion
7.2.1   External Training Feedback Form

The employee must complete the External Training Feedback Form, which captures training relevance, key learning points, immediate application opportunities, and recommended follow-up actions.

7.2.2   Proof of Completion Attachment

The employee attaches proof of external training completion to the ticket. Acceptable proof of completion may include:

  • Certificate of completion, attendance, passing, or certification;
  • Provider-issued attendance report, completion email, transcript, digital badge, or examination result;
  • Screenshot or system-generated completion record from the provider platform, provided that required details are visible;
  • Other official provider confirmation accepted by L&D.

Completion evidence must clearly show, where available, the employee's name, training title, provider name, completion or attendance date, training duration or hours, and result or credential information.

For certifications, licensure, or programs with formal assessment results, the employee shall include the result status and any credential validity period, renewal requirement, or continuing education condition, when applicable.

This step must be completed within three (3) working days. Once completed, the ticket is routed to Step 7.3.

7.3   Documentation Review

The L&D manager reviews the submitted proof of completion within five (5) working days from submission. The review shall confirm whether the submitted document is legible, complete, relevant to the approved training, and sufficient for recordkeeping.

If documentation is incomplete, unclear, or inconsistent with the approved training, the L&D manager shall return the ticket to Step 7.2 with required corrections or additional evidence.

If documentation is valid and complete, the ticket is routed to Step 7.4.

7.4   Proof of Knowledge Transfer

The employee uploads proof of knowledge transfer within twenty (20) working days after the activity. Acceptable proof may include one or more of the following, depending on the approved method:

  • Knowledge Transfer Report or summary of topics covered, key insights, action points, and recommendations;
  • Attendance sheet, participant list, or system attendance report;
  • Presentation deck, job aid, process guide, checklist, recording, or shared learning material;
  • Participant feedback, acknowledgement, short quiz results, discussion notes, or supervisor sign-off;
  • Evidence of application, such as updated work instruction, improvement proposal, coaching log, or implementation action plan.

Proof of knowledge transfer must be sufficient to show that the learning was shared with the intended audience and that participants had an opportunity to receive, discuss, and apply the transferred knowledge.

The ticket is routed to Step 7.5 upon completion of this task.

If knowledge transfer is not required for a particular external training, then proceed to Step 7.6.

7.5   Validate Knowledge Transfer

L&D reviews proof of knowledge transfer within five (5) working days from submission. Validation shall confirm that the activity was conducted, the target audience was covered, materials or outputs were attached, and the proof supports the approved training objectives.

If proof is incomplete, the L&D manager shall return the ticket to Step 7.4 for correction, additional evidence, or rescheduling of the knowledge transfer activity, as applicable.

If the proof is valid and complete, the training record is updated, and the ticket is routed to Step 7.6.1.

7.6   Employee Record Update and Closure
7.6.1   HR Record Update

The HR Specialist acknowledges the submissions and updates employee records.

7.6.2   Dermorepubliq University Update

The employee adds their External Learning Record in Dermorepubliq University.

7.6.3   Ticket Closure

The employee verifies completion and closes the ticket.

Failure to submit complete documentation or proof of knowledge transfer may result in delayed ticket closure, incomplete training records, escalation to the immediate supervisor or Department Head, and other actions in accordance with applicable policies. Repeated non-compliance may be subject to management review.

Records shall be retained in the designated system or repository in accordance with applicable HR and document retention requirements. Documents must be legible, traceable, and accessible to authorized reviewers for audit and reporting purposes.

The L&D Manager is responsible for maintaining and updating this SOP in coordination with HR and relevant stakeholders. This SOP shall be reviewed annually or as needed due to changes in the External Training Policy, documentation requirements, ticketing system workflow, audit findings, or organizational structure. All revisions must be approved, documented, communicated, and stored in the official document repository.
