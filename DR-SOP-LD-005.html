PURPOSE

The purpose of this Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is to establish a structured immersion program that allows employees from non-operational departments to gain firsthand exposure to the company’s production and fulfillment processes. This program aims to improve understanding of operational workflows, enhance cross-department collaboration, and promote appreciation of the roles and responsibilities within the production and fulfillment teams.

2
SCOPE

In Scope

This SOP applies to all newly hired individual contributors and people leaders who are not assigned to the Production, Fulfillment, Quality Services, or Warehouse departments. The immersion activity will take place within the Production and Fulfillment departments as part of the onboarding and orientation process.

Out of Scope

This SOP does not apply to consultants, external partners, and interns.

Process Start and End

This SOP is triggered upon the trainer’s acknowledgement of the HR Onboarding Notification sent by the HR specialists.

This SOP ends upon the HR specialists’ acknowledgement of the Immersion checklist sent by the trainer.

DEFINITIONS
  • Immersion Activity – A structured onboarding activity where employees temporarily participate in or observe operational tasks within designated departments to gain practical understanding of the company’s core processes.
  • Immersion Guide – The designated person on the site who will guide the participants during the immersion activity.

PROCEDURE

Scheduling

Trainer schedules the immersion activity upon receiving the HR Onboarding notification.

Trainer notifies HR, hiring manager, and site managers about the immersion activity and schedule at least three (3) days before the activity.

Site manager informs trainer of the designated immersion guide.

Trainer coordinates with the Logistics department for carpooling services at least three (3) days before the activity.

Immersion Orientation

Trainer briefs the immersion participants at least a day before the activity regarding the schedule, safety guidelines, expectations, prohibited activities, and required personal protective equipment (PPE).

Trainer provides a copy of the LD-TMP-010-1.0-2026 - Immersion Checklist to each participant.

Immersion Proper

Participants report to the assigned department at the scheduled time.

The immersion guide introduces the department’s functions and workflow.

Participants may observe operations and, when appropriate, assist with basic tasks under supervision.

Participants are encouraged to ask questions to understand processes and responsibilities.

The immersion guide checks all tasks in LD-TMP-010-1.0-2026 - Immersion Checklist that were observed or performed.

Post-Immersion Activity

Participants complete the Immersion survey in Dermorepubliq University.

Immersion guide confirms participation and completion of the immersion activity.

Participants submit the signed LD-TMP-010-1.0-2026 - Immersion Checklist to the trainer.

Trainer acknowledges receipt of the signed LD-TMP-010-1.0-2026 - Immersion Checklist and submits this to the HR specialist for recordkeeping.

TRAINING REQUIREMENTS

The immersion guide must have been with the company for at least six (6) months and have exhibited extensive knowledge about the department processes.

10
KPI and COMPLIANCE

Compliance and effectiveness of the Immersion Activity Program will be monitored to ensure that the program achieves its objective of providing new hires with practical exposure to production and fulfillment operations. Monitoring results will be reviewed by the L&D team and relevant department supervisors, and corrective actions will be implemented when performance targets are not met.

If performance targets related to the immersion activity are not achieved, L&D will review the causes and coordinate with department supervisors to implement corrective actions. These actions may include adjusting schedules, improving orientation briefings, or enhancing program coordination to ensure the immersion objectives are met.

Failure of participants to follow safety procedures, operational guidelines, or supervisor instructions during the immersion activity may result in removal from the program and possible disciplinary action in accordance with company policies. Repeated or serious violations will be escalated to Human Resources and department management for appropriate action.
