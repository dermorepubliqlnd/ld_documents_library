This SOP establishes a standardized process for submitting, reviewing, prioritizing, and approving Learning & Development (L&D) project requests. It ensures that all L&D initiatives are aligned with organizational goals, resourced appropriately, and managed consistently.

In Scope

This SOP applies to:

  • All departments requesting support from L&D.
  • All L&D team members involved in evaluating, planning, and executing L&D projects.
  • Requesting of content development and training, up to the approval of the project scope only.
  • Requests related to Dermorepubliq University concerns, document reviews, L&D laptop loan, and training records.
Out of Scope

This SOP does not apply to general inquiries and external training requests. Project management of accepted content development and training requests is out of this SOP's scope.

Process Start and End

This SOP is triggered upon filing of a ticket in the RICA Helpdesk and ends once the assigned agent tags the ticket as complete and closed.

  • Project Intake Request – A formal submission to L&D for new or revised training solutions or initiatives.
  • Requestor – The employee, manager, or department submitting the intake.
  • Assigned Ticket Agent – The person assigned to process a ticket.
  • RICA Helpdesk – A digital platform used to record, classify, and track cross-departmental support requests.
  • Notion – A centralized project management tool used to monitor the status, timelines, deliverables, and progress of active projects. It serves as the single source of truth for project documentation, updates, accountability, and reporting.
  • Triage – Initial assessment of request complexity, alignment, and feasibility.
  • Scoping – Detailed definition of project requirements, deliverables, success metrics, and timelines.
  • Service Level Agreement – A documented commitment outlining the expected turnaround times, response times, responsibilities, and performance standards for L&D services. SLAs help manage expectations, ensure consistent service quality, and define measurable benchmarks for L&D support and project-related processes.

Intake Submission

The requestor files a ticket in the RICA Helpdesk with the following according to the support they need:

Ticket CategoryDescriptionMilestones and Service Level Agreement (SLA)
Content Development Request For requests to create, revise, or update visual, instructional, or communication materials, including but not limited to posters, slide decks, videos, job aids, infographics, handouts, training materials, e-learning content, and other content assets. This category should be used when the primary output is content material or an asset, not the delivery of a training session or program. Triage – 1 business day
Project scoping – 5 business days
Approval of project scope – 2 business days
Internal Training Request For requesting training programs, training sessions, refreshers, onboarding-related training, team-specific learning interventions, or essential and technical skills training. This category should be used when the primary need is for L&D to design, conduct, coordinate, or support a training session or learning program. Triage – 1 business day
Discovery Session – 3 business days
Project scoping – 5 business days
Approval of project scope – 2 business days
Dermorepubliq University Support For Dermorepubliq University-related concerns, including but not limited to profile update, account access, course assignment, e-learning course issues, navigation concerns, report generation, and other system-related support. Address ticket concern – 3 business days
Verify action taken – 2 business days

The request ticket must include the following:

  • Request title – Name of the project or request
  • Requestor – Name of the requestor
  • Requested completion date – Target completion date
  • Department – Requestor's department or business unit
  • Description – Summary of the request
  • Optional file attachment/s – Files or resources to support the request

Please see the appendices section for the other required fields for the content development, training request, and training records tickets.

The requestor submits the accomplished ticket. The ticket is routed to the next step according to its category.

Content Development Request

Ticket is routed to "Triage" milestone. The assigned agent conducts triage using the LD-TMP-011-1.0-2026 - Intake Triage Tool. Based on the results, the assigned agent may:

  • Accept the request. Ticket is approved and routed to the "Project Scoping" milestone (Step 6.2.2).
  • Request revisions from requestor (e.g., incomplete data, unclear objectives).
  • Decline the project due to misalignment or defer the project due to capacity and/or resource constraints. The ticket is rejected and closed.

Only training-related requests will be triaged. Non-training will be assessed based on the availability of resources and team bandwidth. Other solutions will be recommended that may help address the requester's concerns. Recommendations will be recorded on the ticket via comments.

Agent will begin working on the project scope. The agent may set a discovery session with the requester if needed.

L&D Manager reviews project scope.

Agent will revise the project scope according to the L&D manager's feedback. Once all feedback has been addressed, the project scope ticket is marked complete and is routed to "Approval of Project Scope" milestone (Step 6.2.5).

Requestor will review the project scope and provide feedback.

  • If the requestor agrees on the project scope and timeline, the requestor approves and closes the ticket.
  • If the requestor has feedback, the ticket is rejected and routed back to the "Project Scoping" milestone, where the agent will revise the project scope. The ticket is resubmitted and routed to the "Approval of Project Scope" milestone.

Agent updates the team's project tracker. Completion of the actual project will be monitored outside of RICA.

Dermorepubliq University Support

The ticket is routed to "Address ticket concern" milestone (Step 6.3.2).

After addressing the concern or issue, the ticket is routed to the "Verify action taken" milestone (Step 6.3.3).

The requestor reviews and confirms that the concern or issue has been addressed.

  • If the concern has been addressed, the ticket is approved and closed.
  • If the concern has not been addressed or is incomplete, the requestor provides feedback, and the ticket is rejected and routed back to "Address ticket concern milestone". The agent addresses the feedback, and the ticket is routed to the "Verify action taken" milestone.
Document Review

The ticket is routed to "Address ticket concern" milestone (Step 6.4.2).

After addressing the concern or issue, the ticket is routed to the "Verify action taken" milestone (Step 6.4.3).

The requestor reviews and confirms that the concern or issue has been addressed.

  • If the concern has been addressed, the ticket is approved and closed.
  • If the concern has not been addressed or is incomplete, the requestor provides feedback, and the ticket is rejected and routed back to "Address ticket concern milestone". Agent addresses the feedback, and the ticket is routed to the "Verify action taken" milestone.
Training Laptop Loan

The ticket is routed to the "Check Availability of Laptop" milestone. The assigned agent checks the availability of a laptop in the L&D Laptop Tracker.

  • If a laptop is available, the agent checks out the laptop, updates the laptop tracker, and accepts the ticket and routes it to "Acknowledge receipt of Laptop" milestone (Step 6.5.2).
  • If laptops are unavailable, the agent rejects and closes the ticket.

The laptop is endorsed to the requestor. The requestor approves the ticket to acknowledge receipt of the laptop. The ticket is routed to "Laptop Return" milestone (Step 6.5.3).

Requestor returns the laptop on or before the end of the SLA. The agent updates the Laptop Tracker and marks the ticket as complete upon receipt of the laptop. The ticket is routed to the "Verify Ticket Completion" milestone (Step 6.5.4).

Requestor verifies the ticket and marks it as complete. The ticket is closed.

Training Request

Ticket is routed to "Triage" milestone. The assigned agent conducts triage using the LD-TMP-011-1.0-2026 - Intake Triage Tool and assesses the request.

  • If the request is approved, the ticket is routed to the "Discovery Session" milestone (Step 6.6.2).
  • If the request is denied, the agent communicates the decision to the requestor and closes the ticket.

The assigned agent schedules and facilitates a Discovery Session with the requestor to gather training needs information. After the Discovery Session, the agent routes the ticket to the "Project Scoping" milestone (Step 6.6.3).

The assigned agent drafts the project scope and timeline and routes the ticket to the "L&D Review of Project Scope" milestone (Step 6.6.4).

L&D Manager and director review project scope. The agent will revise project scope according to L&D manager and director's feedback. Once all feedback has been addressed, the "Project Scoping" milestone is marked complete and is routed to the "Approval of Project Scope" milestone (Step 6.6.7).

The requestor will review the project scope and provide feedback.

  • If the requestor agrees on the project scope and timeline, the requestor approves and closes the ticket.
  • If the requestor has feedback, the ticket is rejected and routed back to the "Project Scoping" milestone, where the agent will revise the project scope. The ticket is resubmitted and routed to the "Approval of Project Scope" milestone.

Agent updates the team's project tracker.

Training Records Request

The ticket is routed to "Address ticket concern" milestone (Step 6.7.2). After addressing the concern or issue, the training record is attached to the ticket. It is then routed to the "Verify training record" milestone (Step 6.7.3).

The requestor reviews and confirms that they received the training record requested.

  • If the concern has been addressed, the ticket is approved and closed.
  • If the concern has not been addressed or is incomplete, the requestor provides feedback, and the ticket is rejected and routed back to "Address ticket concern milestone". Agent addresses the feedback, and the ticket is routed to the "Verify action taken" milestone.

This section defines how adherence to the L&D Project Intake Request SOP and the effectiveness of the intake process will be monitored. Monitoring ensures that all L&D project requests follow the established intake process, are evaluated consistently, and support effective prioritization and resource allocation. KPI results will be reviewed regularly, and corrective actions will be implemented when targets are not achieved.

Failure to meet KPI targets indicates gaps in adherence to the intake process or inefficiencies in request management. When targets are missed, the L&D Lead will review process bottlenecks, identify root causes, and implement corrective actions such as process adjustments, resource allocation changes, or additional guidance to stakeholders.

Any L&D project initiated without a formally submitted and approved intake request will be considered non-compliant with this SOP. Non-compliant activities may be paused until proper documentation and approval are completed, and repeated violations may be escalated to department leadership for resolution.
